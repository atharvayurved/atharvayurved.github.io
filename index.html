Panchakarma

Panchakarma is a Sanskrit word, meaning "five actions" or "five treatments". It is 'Shodhan' (detoxification) method of treatment. In this process, various Ayurvedic methods are used to detoxify the body and vitalated doshas are removed. It is primarily done for prevention of diseases. It includes

  • Snehan-Swedan: It is a pre-karma for Panchakarma. In this process, gentle massage is done using medicated oil and swedan is done by medicated kwath steams.
  • Vamana (therapeutic vomiting or emesis): This treatment is done for elimination of kapha (cough) and kapha prominent diseases.
  • Virechana: In this treatment, toxins are eliminated via the anal route in pitta prominent diseases.
  • Basti (enema): This treatment is done for vata prominent diseases. In this treatment, medicated kwaths (decoction) and oils are inserted through the anal route. There are different types of enemas such as Matra basti, Madhutailik basti, Asthapana basti and Kshir basti etc. for different purposes.
  • Raktamokshan (blood-letting using leeches and syringes): In this treatment, impure blood is removed from the body using leeches or syringes.
  • Nasya (elimination of toxins through the nose): In this treatment, medicated oils and powders are inserted through the nasal route for treating ENT(ear, nose, throat) diseases.

Kshar Sutra

This treatment is done for anorectal diseases such as piles, fissure, and fistula. In this treatment, piles is ligated with the help of medicated threads called Kshar sutra. The benefit of this treatment is no recurrence of the diseases.

Yoga & Meditation

Ayurveda believes that illness can occur physically as well as mentally. Physical illness can be cured with the help of medications. But mental illness such as stress, strain, anxiety and depression, are treated with Yoga.

Prakriti Parikshan

Ayurveda defines 3 factors with respect to the the health of a human being: physical, mental and spiritual. In physical health, if we know our body constitute called prakriti then we can take an appropriate diet (ahar) and perform the activities accordingly. There are 6 types of prakritis defined in Ayurveda.

Nadi Parikshan

Nadi Pariksha is the ancient ayurvedic technique of diagnosis through the pulse. It can accurately diagnose physical, mental and emotional imbalances as well as diseases. In Ayurveda, three types of nadis are defined namely hans nadi, sarpa nadi and manduk nadi.
